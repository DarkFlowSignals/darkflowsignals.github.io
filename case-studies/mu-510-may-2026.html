The setup

Micron's bull case is structural: HBM (high-bandwidth memory) is the constrained input for every AI accelerator on the market, and Micron is winning the design qualification battle for next-gen HBM4. The cyclical risk is that memory has historically been a commodity. The structural picture held through Q1 2026 because AI demand kept HBM allocation tight regardless of broader memory cycle.

On April 28, the tape signature changed. Within the first two hours of trade, 18 institutional buy-blocks over $500K premium hit the May call chain. Twelve of those cleared the golden-block tier (over $1M premium each). Pattern was identical to the pre-Q3 NVDA print where HBM tightness was the rumor. The market was front-running AMD's earnings on the assumption that strong AMD datacenter commentary would readthrough to MU on Wed open.

The contract choice

Two strikes fired the same morning:

  • $500 call (primary) at $31.60/contract - deeper ITM, higher conviction expression, more delta capture
  • $510 call (watchlist) at $26.77/contract - one strike higher, slightly cheaper, secondary expression

Both on the May 8 expiry, putting AMD's print squarely inside the contract window. The 5/8 expiry was deliberate: it forces the position to resolve on the AMD readthrough either way. No theta-decay tail past the binary.

What happened

  • Mon 4/28: $500C and $510C fire on the morning flow burst. Spot $522.
  • Wed 4/30: $520C 5/15 size-up on continued flow strength.
  • Late April: spot rallies through $560 then $578.
  • 5/4 close: $510C peak +206%, current +160%. Greeks: delta 0.91, theta -$1.31/day (~1.84% daily decay), IV 96.78%.
  • Tue 5/5 AH: AMD earnings - the binary that resolves the position direction.
  • Thu 5/8: contract expiry.

The math at peak

By May 4 close, the trim case became mathematical. With delta 0.91, theta around $1.31/day, and IV at 96.78%, the position would lose ~14% of premium to mechanics alone if held through the AMD print regardless of direction. That's because IV crush on a binary catalyst typically halves the implied vol post-print, and that vega exposure crushes premium even on a flat-direction outcome.

The published exit rule is -50% only. But the trim discipline (taking partial off the table when math says mechanics are about to eat the gain) is the operator-side judgment that keeps the open peak from giving back to the public stop. Members watched the per-Greek numbers update every 5 minutes inside the dashboard expand panel.

The thesis structure

  • Setup: bullish call entry on a tape-reader flag - 18 institutional buy-blocks over $500K premium in two hours
  • Catalyst: AMD earnings AH 5/5 is the proximate catalyst; an AMD beat with strong datacenter commentary cascades into MU readthrough Wed open
  • Macro: tightening tape pressures growth multiples, but semi memory has been the AI proxy of choice for institutional exposure that refuses to chase the apex names
  • Risk: stop at $13.39 (50% of entry premium); 4d DTE makes theta dominant; AMD miss with bearish HBM commentary invalidates
Trim discipline is the answer to "what do you do when the position runs ahead of plan?" The position rides; the operator's job is to take partial off when the mechanics turn against you. The published rule still closes at -50% or expiry, nothing else.

What this case shows

Tape-reader fires are different from continuation fires. A tape-reader fire is when something genuinely new appears in the flow data - a fresh pattern that wasn't there yesterday. The 18-block burst on MU 4/28 was that. Members got the entry the moment the pattern crossed the conviction bar, not after the cohort had already moved.

Combined with the structural HBM thesis (Micron specifically winning HBM4 qualification), the position had both the tape signature and the fundamental case. That's the kind of agreement across signals the system looks for.
